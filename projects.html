Cognitive Neuroscience-Driven Advancements in Machine Learning Algorithms

The project applies cognitive neuroscience principles to enhance AI systems. By leveraging concepts like brain plasticity, it allows neural networks to adapt and learn from experience, similar to human learning. This approach improves AI's ability to recognize and interpret complex patterns and nuances. Techniques used include deep learning architectures, recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and cognitive-inspired algorithms. This research, a finalist in the International Research Project Olympiad (IRPrO), aims to advance human-AI interactions and applications in various sectors, including healthcare and autonomous systems.

Plastic But Fantastic!

The project is a semi-circular tunnel designed to address Egypt’s urban congestion, pollution, and climate change challenges. The tunnel utilizes plastic polymers and has the capability to support 118 kilograms with a real-life strength factor of 70x. Its semi-circular design optimizes load distribution, utilizing structural mechanics principles to manage pressure across vertical, horizontal, and radial axes. The design addresses both compressive and tensile stresses, ensuring stability and durability. This project, which received a 95% rating from experts, including professors from Cairo University, showcases advanced engineering and material science.



DermDetect

Dermdetect utilizes AI to analyze skin concerns through photos. This means the app leverages advanced algorithms to examine your images, potentially similar to how a dermatologist would with specialized training. This technology can improve accessibility to skin checks and potentially save lives by enabling: Early detection: By identifying suspicious moles or lesions early, Dermdetect could help catch skin cancer in its most treatable stages. Remote screening: The app allows for initial assessments without needing an in-person visit, potentially reducing wait times and increasing access to dermatological care, especially in underserved areas.
